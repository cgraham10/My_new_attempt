Canadian Food Inspection Agency

Project Overview

The Problem:
We discovered that government websites, including the Canadian Food Inspection Agency, can overwhelm people due to unclear navigation and the large amount of information housed on the webpage.

The Solution:
We believe that there is an opportunity to provide users with an easier navigation system and clean up the amount of information housed on the homepage.

Tools:
Miro, Google Workplace, Figma, Slack, Zoom

My Responsibilities:
UX Research, Ideation, Prototyping, User Testing, Iterations.

User Research

User Path/Usability Testing:
A total of 6 usability test were conducted to determine how easy it was for the user to navigate the website.

The Objectives:
Can users successfully discover if their dog is able to travel with them to Canada?

Results:

Goal/Output:
See if your dog is eligible to enter Canada

Assumptions:
User plans to travel to Canada and has a dog over 8 months

Steps:

    1. Navigate to Canada Food Inspection Agency
    2. Find the information link for “traveling with pets
    3. Select “traveling with pets
    4. Find “requirements for bringing an animal into Canada
    5. Fill out form for dog over 8 months

Success Criteria:
Successfully discovered if their dog is able to enter Canada

Priority Matrix:

The data taken from the usability testing was then move into an Priority Matrix.

From here the data was grouped based on the following:

    1. Most Important to Users
    2. Least Important to Users
    3. Most Important to Agency
    4. Least Important ot Agency

Results:

Most Important to User:

    1. Easy to Navigate
    2. Clean and simple
    3. Helpful information

Least Important to User:

    1. Bad stock photos
    2. "What traveler's can bring" should be split out
    3. Pet choices should be alphabetical

Most Important to Agency:

    1. Likes the detail information provided
    2. Grouping areas that could be better categorized (food, animal, plant)
    3. Very simple website.

Least Important to Agency:

    1. Maybe some pictures to go along with the links so it’s more relevant
    2. Lots of margin space
    3. Good site for average people

Homepage Annotations

1. Menu:
The menu bar on this site is directed towards the entire Canadian government but it is not relevant to the CFIA Website. Currently there is no Navigation bar for the CFIA. Since this is global navigation, we should move it to the top of the web page. A new Navigation bar needs to be created to help the user work through the CFIA website specifically.

2. CFIA-Sepcific Information:
These sections should be converted into a navigation/menu bar for the CFIA. The can be sorted based on similarities. Using card sorting we will break down L1/L2 content within the new navigation bar to better sort the content.

3. Most Requested:
This can be removed or possibly moved to pop up as suggested search options when the user clicks the “Search” bar.

Lean Usability Testing Plan
